Welcome!

I am Jihye Lee. I am an Assistant Professor of Social Work at the Brown School at Washington University in St. Louis.

University LinkedIn Google Scholar ResearchGate Email

My research aims to advance behavioral health equity and promote well-being in digital contexts among immigrant and ethnic minority communities through the application of data-driven and computational methods. I further investigate how contexts and experiences, particularly structural racism or immigration-related stressors, impact well-being. Through my research, I aim to contribute to the development of inclusive, equitable, and culturally-responsive interventions.

Broadly, my substantive focuses on three interconnected areas:

  1. Racism, anti-Asian hate, Latinx anti-immigrant sentiment towards immigrant populations and minority utilization among immigrant and ethnic minority populations
  2. Social context and contextual health intervention grounded in addressing population health, particularly in understanding communities online
  3. Online application of data science methodologies to social work and mental health services research, including natural language processing, geospatial analytics, and the use of large-scale administrative and digital media data

I am motivated to use mixed-methods, multimodal approaches to unite my data science, social justice & diversity, minority of the minority for Social Work and Research, and Social Health. My dissertation research has been awarded and funded by Social Psychology for Social Work, the Korean Psychological Association, and the Augusto Scholars Program. I have published political research and psychosocial surveys from the Augusto Research and Training Center.

Prior to joining Washington University in St. Louis, I completed my research doctoral program in Social Work from the University of Michigan School of Social Work and was a Social Justice Administrative to the Clinical Work Expert, the Department of Policy Lab in Research. My CV is available here.
